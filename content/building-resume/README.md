## Building resume

### **Overview**

In today's competitive job market, your resume is often the first impression a recruiter has of you. This project will guide you in crafting a structured, compelling, and professional resume that clearly communicates your skills, experiences, and goals. Your final deliverable will serve as both a personal branding tool and a requirement for later employability projects.

### **Role Play**

You are applying for your dream internship or junior position in a competitive tech company. The recruiter will not meet you unless your resume stands out from hundreds of other applications. Your job is to craft a professional resume that captures attention and highlights your potential.

### **Learning Objective**

By the end of this project, you will be able to:

- Structure a resume using widely accepted formats such as reverse-chronological or functional.
- Identify and describe key achievements, skills, and experiences relevant to your target role.
- Apply keyword optimization techniques to align with applicant tracking systems (ATS).
- Collaborate with peers to refine and validate resumes using constructive feedback.
- Develop self-awareness and communication skills through reflective resume building.

### **Timeline & Effort**

- **Maximum Duration:** 15 days from start to completion

### **Detailed Process**

1. **Research target roles and gather materials**

- Research 1-2 job types that interest you
- Gather your past experiences, education, projects, and achievements

2. **Draft your first resume version**

- Choose your format and create initial draft using templates or from scratch

3. **Peer review session**

- Share your resume with peers and collect structured feedback

4. **Revise and polish**

- Integrate feedback and refine your layout/design

5. **Final preparation**

- Final proofreading, PDF export, and reflection submission

### **Instructions**

1. Define your professional objective and the role you are targeting.
2. Choose a resume format (reverse-chronological, functional, or hybrid).
3. List your relevant experiences, education, and skills with measurable results when possible.
4. Use resume builders or structured templates to format your resume.
5. Optimize your resume using ATS-friendly tools and industry-specific keywords.
6. Submit your resume for peer validation and integrate their feedback.
7. Prepare your final version for submission, ensuring consistency in layout and clarity.

**Bonus Challenge**: Create two versions of your resume (one general and one customized for a specific job description).

### **Tips**

- Avoid long paragraphs; use concise bullet points.
- Start your bullet points with action verbs (e.g., "Developed," "Led," "Optimized").
- Tailor your resume to each job offer by matching skills and terminology.
- Keep your design clean and professional (avoid excessive colors or visuals).
- Peer reviews help spot blind spots (always ask for outside input from your peers).

### **Deliverables**

Submit your final resume as a PDF file with the following naming convention:
**`Resume_FullName.pdf`**

**Example:** `Resume_JohnSmith.pdf`

### **Repository Structure**

Create a repository named `resume-building` with the following structure:

```
resume-building/
├── README.md
└── Resume_FullName.pdf
```

**README.md Requirements:**
Your README.md file should document your research process and include:

1. **Target Role Research**
   - 1-2 job types you researched
   - Key job descriptions you analyzed
   - Common requirements and qualifications identified

2. **Format Selection Rationale**
   - Which resume format you chose (reverse-chronological, functional, or hybrid)
   - Why this format best suits your profile and target role

3. **ATS Optimization Strategy**
   - Industry-specific keywords you incorporated
   - Tools used for optimization (Jobscan, ResyMatch, etc.)
   - How you tailored content for ATS compatibility

4. **Peer Review Summary**
   - Key feedback received during peer review session
   - Changes made based on peer input
   - Final improvements implemented

5. **Reflection**
   - What you learned about your professional profile
   - Challenges faced during the resume building process
   - How this resume aligns with your career goals

### **Submission Structure**

Your resume must include the following sections in a logical order:

**Required Sections:**

1. **Header** - Full name, professional email, phone number, LinkedIn profile (and portfolio/GitHub if relevant)
2. **Professional Summary/Objective** - 2-3 sentence overview of your career goals and key qualifications
3. **Experience** - Work history with company names, job titles, dates, and bullet-pointed achievements
4. **Education** - Degrees, institutions, graduation dates, relevant coursework/honors
5. **Skills** - Technical skills, programming languages, tools, and relevant soft skills
6. **Projects** (if applicable) - Relevant academic, personal, or professional projects with brief descriptions

**Additional Sections**

- Certifications
- Awards and Honors
- Volunteer Experience

### **File Requirements:**

- **Format:** PDF only
- **Size:** Maximum 2MB
- **Length:** 1-2 pages recommended
- **Font:** Professional fonts only (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, etc.)
- **Font Size:** 10-12pt for body text, 14-16pt for headers
- **Margins:** 0.5-1 inch on all sides
